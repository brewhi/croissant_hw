Croissant

The croissant is a popular French pastry that is recognized by the timeconsuming and tedius process of laminating the bread dough in order to create it's delicate flaky layers. Laminating dough is the process of folding butter into dough many times, which creates multiple alternating layers of butter and dough. When the laminated dough bakes, the butter melts and creates steam. This steam lifts the layers apart, leaving us with dozens of flaky airy buttery layers.
